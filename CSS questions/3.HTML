About W3C

The World Wide Web Consortium (W3C) is an international community that develops open standards to ensure the long-term growth of the Web. It was founded by Sir Tim Berners-Lee and is dedicated to leading the Web to its full potential by creating technical specifications and guidelines that promote its evolution and ensure its interoperability.

Mission

The mission of W3C is to lead the World Wide Web to its full potential by developing protocols and guidelines that ensure the long-term growth of the Web.

Standards

W3C develops open standards to ensure the interoperability, accessibility, and evolution of the Web. These standards cover various aspects of web technologies, including HTML, CSS, XML, and more.

Contributions

W3C's contributions have significantly shaped the modern web. It has played a crucial role in the development of web standards and technologies, enabling a consistent and accessible online experience for users worldwide.
